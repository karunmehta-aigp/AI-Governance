## Purpose

Before any deeper EU AI Act work begins, use this assessment to determine (a) whether the Act applies to your organisation at all, and (b) which role(s) you hold under it. Most compliance programmes waste early effort by skipping straight to risk classification without first confirming the Act applies and in what capacity.

## Section A — Territorial and Material Scope

Answer yes/no to each. Any "yes" means the Act's scope likely captures your organisation.

| Question | Yes/No |
|---|---|
| Does your organisation place an AI system on the EU market, or put one into service in the EU, regardless of where your organisation is established? | |
| Does your organisation (established outside the EU) have an AI system whose output is used in the EU? | |
| Is your organisation a provider or deployer of an AI system established in the EU? | |
| Does your organisation import or distribute AI systems into the EU market? | |

If all answers are "No," the Act likely does not apply — but re-check this assessment if your market footprint changes.

## Section B — Determine Your Role(s)

An organisation can hold more than one role simultaneously, including for different systems.

| Role | Definition | Does this apply to you? |
|---|---|---|
| **Provider** | Develops an AI system (or has one developed) and places it on the market or puts it into service under its own name/trademark | |
| **Deployer** | Uses an AI system under its own authority, other than in the course of a personal non-professional activity | |
| **Importer** | Established in the EU, places on the market an AI system bearing the name/trademark of an entity established outside the EU | |
| **Distributor** | Makes an AI system available on the EU market, other than as provider or importer | |
| **Product manufacturer** | Places a product on the market together with an AI system under its own name/trademark | |

## Section C — Is the System Even "AI" Under the Act's Definition?

The Act defines an AI system as a machine-based system that infers, from inputs, how to generate outputs (predictions, content, recommendations, or decisions) that can influence physical or virtual environments, and that operates with varying levels of autonomy and may exhibit adaptiveness after deployment.

| Question | Yes/No |
|---|---|
| Does the system use machine learning, or an inference-based approach, rather than purely deterministic if/then logic? | |
| Does the system produce outputs (predictions, content, recommendations, decisions) rather than simply displaying stored data? | |
| Does the system operate with some degree of autonomy from direct human instruction on each output? | |

If a system is purely rule-based automation with no learned or probabilistic component, it likely falls outside the Act's definition — but should still be logged in the AI System Inventory if it materially affects individuals.

## Section D — Initial Prohibited Practice Screen

Any "yes" here requires immediate escalation to Legal — do not proceed with deployment.

| Question | Yes/No |
|---|---|
| Does the system perform social scoring of individuals leading to detrimental treatment unrelated to the context in which the data was generated? | |
| Does the system use subliminal or manipulative techniques materially distorting behaviour in a way that causes harm? | |
| Does the system perform real-time remote biometric identification in publicly accessible spaces for law enforcement (absent a narrow legal exemption)? | |
| Does the system infer emotions in workplace or education settings (absent a safety or medical exemption)? | |
