## Purpose

Satisfies ISO/IEC 42001 Clause 10.2 — the procedure for reacting to nonconformities and taking corrective action, ensuring root causes are addressed rather than only symptoms.

## 2. Immediate Reaction

1. Evaluate the need for immediate action to control and correct the nonconformity.
2. Contain any ongoing impact (e.g. pause the affected process or system if risk is active).
3. Deal with the consequences, including any harm caused.

## 3. Root Cause Analysis

Do not stop at the proximate cause. Use a structured method (e.g. 5 Whys) to trace the nonconformity to its underlying cause — a process gap, a training gap, a resourcing gap, or a design flaw in the AIMS itself.
