## 1. Purpose

The AI Governance Committee (the "Committee") is the senior cross-functional body accountable for overseeing the organisation's responsible development, procurement, and use of artificial intelligence. This Charter defines the Committee's mandate, composition, decision rights, and operating rhythm.

## 2. Mandate

- Approve and periodically review the AI Governance Framework and AI Policy.
- Set the organisation's AI risk appetite and approve the risk classification model.
- Approve deployment of high-risk AI systems prior to production go-live.
- Review and approve remediation plans arising from AI incidents or audit findings.
- Escalation point for disputes on risk classification, use case approval, or resourcing of governance activity.
- Report AI governance program status to executive leadership and, where applicable, the Board risk committee.

## 3. Membership

| Role | Committee function | Voting |
|---|---|---|
| Chief AI Officer / Chief Data Officer (Chair) | Chairs meetings, sets agenda, casts tie-breaking vote | Yes |
| General Counsel / Legal representative | Regulatory interpretation, contractual and liability review | Yes |
| Chief Risk Officer / Risk representative | Enterprise risk alignment, risk appetite input | Yes |
| Data Protection Officer / Privacy Lead | Data governance, DPIA/FRIA sign-off input | Yes |
| Chief Information Security Officer | Security and adversarial risk review | Yes |
| Business Unit Leads (rotating, per agenda) | Business context for use cases under review | No — advisory |
| AI Governance Office Lead (Secretariat) | Prepares papers, maintains minutes and action log, tracks the inventory | No — non-voting |
| Internal Audit (observer) | Independent observation, no operational role | No — observer |

## 4. Decision Rights

| Decision | Authority |
|---|---|
| Approve/reject high-risk system for production deployment | Committee majority vote |
| Approve AI Policy and Framework updates | Committee majority vote, ratified by executive sponsor |
| Approve exceptions to AI Policy | Committee majority vote, time-bound with remediation plan |
| Classify a system as limited or minimal risk | AI Governance Office, with Committee spot-audit rights |
| Authorise emergency suspension of a live AI system | Chair + CISO or Chair + General Counsel, ratified at next meeting |
| Close out a governance incident | AI Governance Office recommendation, Committee sign-off for high-severity incidents |

## 5. Operating Rhythm

- Standing meeting: monthly, minimum 10 members' notice, quorum = 5 voting members including the Chair or delegate.
- Ad hoc sessions convened within 5 business days for high-risk approval requests or Severity 1 incidents.
- Quarterly deep-dive: portfolio-wide review of the AI System Inventory, Quarterly Governance Review submissions, and maturity assessment trend.
- Annual session: review of the Framework, Policy, risk appetite statement, and regulatory horizon-scan.

## 6. Secretariat Responsibilities

1. Distribute agenda and papers at least 5 business days before each meeting.
2. Maintain the official minute record, decision log, and action tracker.
3. Maintain version control of the Framework, Policy, and Charter.
4. Track open actions to closure and report status at each meeting.

## 7. Reporting Lines

The Committee reports to the [Executive Risk Committee / Board Risk Committee — organisation-specific] on a quarterly basis, summarising: inventory size and risk tier distribution, high-risk approvals granted or declined, incidents and remediation status, maturity assessment trend, and material regulatory developments.

## 8. Conflicts of Interest

Members must declare any commercial, vendor, or personal interest relevant to an agenda item. Where a conflict exists, the member may contribute to discussion but is excluded from the related vote, recorded in the minutes.

## 9. Charter Review

This Charter is reviewed annually by the Committee and approved by its executive sponsor. Material changes to membership, decision rights, or reporting lines require executive sponsor approval.
