## 1. Purpose

This document describes a phased, four-quarter approach to standing up an AI governance program from a zero or low baseline. It is designed to be read alongside the companion workbook (`07-Implementation-Roadmap.xlsx`), which tracks phase tasks, owners, dates, and status.

## 2. Phasing Overview

| Phase | Timeframe | Primary objective |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 — Foundation | Weeks 1–6 | Establish governance structure, approve Framework and Policy, begin inventory build |
| Phase 2 — Discovery & Classification | Weeks 5–12 | Complete AI system inventory, classify by risk tier, identify high-risk backlog |
| Phase 3 — Controls Build-Out | Weeks 10–20 | Stand up conformity documentation process, human oversight procedures, incident response |
| Phase 4 — Operationalise & Mature | Weeks 18–26 and ongoing | Quarterly review cadence, maturity assessment baseline, committee reporting rhythm |

## 3. Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–6)

- Secure executive sponsor and confirm AI Governance Committee membership.
- Draft and approve the AI Governance Framework and AI Policy (Committee sign-off).
- Stand up the AI Governance Office (may initially be a single accountable lead).
- Communicate the Policy organisation-wide and open the intake channel for new AI use cases.
- Begin populating the AI System Inventory from known systems and procurement records.

## 4. Phase 2 — Discovery & Classification (Weeks 5–12)

- Run a structured discovery exercise: procurement records, IT asset register, vendor contract review, and a business-unit self-declaration survey to surface shadow AI.
- Complete AI Use Case Assessment Forms for all discovered systems.
- Apply the risk classification model; flag unacceptable-risk use for immediate remediation.
- Build the initial high-risk backlog and sequence it for Phase 3 conformity work.

## 5. Phase 3 — Controls Build-Out (Weeks 10–20)

- Develop conformity documentation templates (technical documentation, risk management file, human oversight procedure, instructions for use) for high-risk systems.
- Stand up the incident response and regulatory escalation procedure, including severity definitions and escalation paths.
- Define human oversight control points for each high-risk system and confirm named override authority.
- Deliver initial training to business owners, technical owners, and the Committee on their governance responsibilities.

## 6. Phase 4 — Operationalise & Mature (Weeks 18–26+)

- Launch the quarterly governance review cycle for high- and limited-risk systems.
- Run the first AI Governance Maturity Assessment to establish a baseline score across domains.
- Establish the Committee's standing quarterly reporting package to executive leadership.
- Set a 6–12 month re-assessment cycle to track maturity progression and refresh the roadmap for the next horizon.

## 7. Critical Success Factors

- Executive sponsorship visible from Phase 1 — governance stalls without a mandate from the top.
- Proportionate controls — over-engineering minimal-risk system requirements erodes business buy-in.
- Single source of truth — the inventory must be the authoritative record referenced by procurement, legal, and audit.
- Iterative rollout — Phase 3 controls can be piloted on 1–2 high-risk systems before scaling organisation-wide.

## 8. How to Use the Companion Workbook

The workbook (`07-Implementation-Roadmap.xlsx`) breaks each phase into discrete tasks with owner, target start/end dates, dependency notes, and a status field (Not Started / In Progress / Blocked / Complete). Progress percentage is calculated automatically from task status. Update the workbook at each Committee meeting to keep the roadmap current.
