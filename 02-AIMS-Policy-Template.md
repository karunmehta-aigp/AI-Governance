## Purpose

Satisfies ISO/IEC 42001 Clause 5.2 — the AI policy that top management must establish for the AI Management System (AIMS). This is distinct from the operational AI Policy in the core Governance Toolkit: this document is the *management-system-level* policy statement an auditor checks against during certification.

## 1. Policy Statement

[Organisation name] establishes, implements, and maintains an AI Management System conforming to ISO/IEC 42001:2023, to ensure the responsible, trustworthy, and continually improving development, provision, and use of artificial intelligence across the organisation.

## 2. Commitments

Top management commits to:

- Complying with applicable legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements relating to AI systems.
- Providing a framework for setting and reviewing AI objectives.
- Ensuring the availability of resources needed for the AIMS.
- Communicating the importance of effective AI management to all relevant roles.
- Ensuring the AIMS achieves its intended outcomes.
- Promoting continual improvement of the AIMS.
- Supporting other relevant management roles to demonstrate leadership within their areas of responsibility.

## 3. Scope Reference

This policy applies within the AIMS scope defined in the Context of the Organization Worksheet (ISO-003). It covers all AI systems developed, procured, or operated by [organisation name] within that scope.

## 4. Alignment with Organisational Values

- Human-centred values and fairness in AI system design and use.
- Transparency and explainability appropriate to context.
- Robustness, safety, and security throughout the AI lifecycle.
- Accountability for AI system outcomes at every stage.

These align directly with the OECD AI Principles and are operationalised through the risk management, data governance, and human oversight requirements in the wider AI Governance Toolkit.

| Role | AIMS Responsibility |
